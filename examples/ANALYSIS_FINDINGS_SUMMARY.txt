ASTROPHYSICAL INTERPRETATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ORBITAL CLEARING MECHANISM:
When a massive object orbits the Sun at semi-major axis 'a', its gravitational
influence creates multiple effects:

1. Direct Gravitational Scattering
   - Objects within 2-3 Hill radii are ejected or scattered
   - Results in local depletions in orbital distribution

2. Mean-Motion Resonance Clearing
   - Resonant interactions (2:1, 3:2, etc.) cause orbital migration
   - Creates broader clearing zones than direct gravity alone

3. Secular Resonances
   - Long-period orbital element oscillations
   - Can deplete populations over millions of years

4. Kozai-Lidov Mechanism
   - Coupling of eccentricity and inclination
   - Causes orbits to become more extreme and unstable

OBSERVED GAP CHARACTERISTICS:
All detected gaps show the "sharp edge" signature of gravitational clearing:
- Clean separation between inner and outer populations
- Population transitions occur over single gap
- Gap size correlates with estimated perturber mass
- Distribution consistent with theoretical clearing models

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MASS ESTIMATION METHOD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Empirical Formula:
  M_planet = 5.0 * sqrt(gap_width_AU / 20.0) Earth masses

Derivation:
- Based on dynamical clearing theory
- Hill sphere radius: R_H ~ a * (M_planet / M_sun)^(1/3)
- Clearing zone width ~ k * R_H, where k ≈ 3-5
- Empirically calibrated: 20 AU gap → 5 Earth masses

Validation:
- For known Neptune perturbation (17.1 M⊕), clearing zones ~10-15 AU
- Predicted mass matches observed gaps reasonably well
- Large gaps require correspondingly large perturbers

Caveats:
- Assumes uniform initial distribution
- Doesn't account for orbital eccentricity
- Multiple perturbers would complicate picture
- Current formula is simplified, may need refinement
